  • Children of Àṣẹ

    My senior year at UC Santa Cruz entailed a year-long series of classes centering about the development of a polished game: Children of Àṣẹ, a top-down social dynamics RPG set within the Yoruba culture of what today is Nigeria. I was one of nine developers, working with a core team of around five artists – a total of 27 people contributed in all. I handled much of the OpenGL rendering, asset integration, and tutorial design. We spent twenty weeks in all on the game, divided into two-week sprints and holding Scrum three times each week.

  • Daedra-tasitc Rune Spells

    A mod made for Skyrim: Special Edition to replace the textures used by the rune spells, cast on a surface to deal damage or confer an effect when an enemy comes near. I made the textures myself in GIMP, and exported diffuse, normal, and parallax maps as BC7 .dds images. I then wrote an XML installation script to choose resolution options and optional add-ons, and authored the Nexus Mods page. The mod and all its components are released under the CC-BY 4.0 license.

    Visit the Nexus Mods page

  • OpenGov

    OpenGov is a Ruby on Rails project for scraping a parsing publicly available government statistics data into a queryable PostqreSQL database. As part of a team of four, I helped design the scraping and parsing logic to convert tabular data into a JSON-like format for storage. This took place over the course of five weeks, with an agile development cycle comprising one-week sprints.

  • Rosetta Run

    An endless runner game played directly from the browser, designed with the Phaser Javascript game engine. Inspired by the European Space Agency's 2014 Rosetta mission, the player avoids various space-themed obstacles and gathers "data samples" for score. Alongside a team of three others, I implemented movement, animation, and obstacle spawning mechanics. Our graphical assets were each hand-drawn on paper, then scaned and digitally traced.
