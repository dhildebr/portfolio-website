An endless runner game played directly from the browser, designed with the Phaser Javascript game engine. Inspired by the European Space Agency's 2014 Rosetta mission, the player avoids various space-themed obstacles and gathers "data samples" for score. Alongside a team of three others, I implemented movement, animation, and obstacle spawning mechanics. Our graphical assets were each hand-drawn on paper, then scanned and digitally traced.

You can play Rosetta Run right here in your browser. Note that the game is a fixed 400×600 pixels, and relies on the player having a traditional keyboard and mouse. Accordingly, it (and this page) may not cooperate with tablets of phones.
